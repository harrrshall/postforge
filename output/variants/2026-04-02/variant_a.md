## Post Text

97% of Indian SMB leaders say they use AI.

The real number doing anything meaningful with it? Under 5%.

LinkedIn's own 2026 report says 97% of Indian SMBs "use AI." NASSCOM-EY says fewer than 4% have any real AI expertise. Both are right. They're just measuring different things.

"Using AI" in 2026 India means asking ChatGPT to rewrite an email. That's not automation. That's a fancy spellcheck.

An AI agent that answers your patients' WhatsApp messages at 2 AM, books appointments, sends payment reminders, and has a report ready by 7 AM — that's a different universe.

78.3 million registered MSMEs in India. $500 billion in potential economic value sitting on the table, according to the WEF.

And almost nobody's picking it up.

Not because they don't want to. 59% say budget is the barrier. 72% say they need training. The tools built for Fortune 500 companies cost Fortune 500 money.

A Pune clinic owner doesn't need a $200K enterprise AI stack. She needs an AI employee on WhatsApp that costs less than a part-time receptionist.

That's ₹4,999/month. Not ₹50 lakhs/year.

The gap between "I use AI" and "AI runs part of my business" is where the real opportunity lives.

Which side are you on — and what's actually stopping you from crossing over?
