# IndusLaw (CMS INDUSLAW)

> **Current Brand:** CMS INDUSLAW — since the firm joined the CMS international organisation in May 2025, it operates under the dual trade names "INDUSLAW" and "CMS INDUSLAW." It remains an independent Indian law firm.

---

## Firm Overview

CMS INDUSLAW is a full-service Indian law firm founded in 2000 and headquartered in Bengaluru. It is the **7th largest law firm in India** by headcount, with approximately **450 lawyers** and **60+ partners** across six office cities. The firm advises domestic and international clients on Indian law across transactional, regulatory, advisory, and dispute resolution matters.

## Organizational Hierarchy

CMS INDUSLAW operates a **democratic partnership model** with no single Managing Partner. Decision-making is distributed across the founding and senior partners. The firm explicitly uses a flat hierarchical approach in which "every partner is given an equal opportunity to keep their point."

### Tier Structure (Internal)

```
Founding & Senior Partners (4)
    └── Senior Partner (non-founding)
        └── Equity Partners
            └── Partners
                └── Counsels / Principal Associates
                    └── Senior Associates
                        └── Associates
                            └── Trainee Associates / Interns
```

### Internal Committees (confirmed)

- Knowledge Management Committee
- Training Committee

Associates participate in committee work, providing a structured pathway for career development.

### The Firm's Governance Philosophy

The founding partners deliberately structured the firm to avoid the "best friend" trap common in Indian partnerships — they maintain multi-firm relationships across jurisdictions rather than exclusive alliances. Partner evaluation occurs holistically **every three years**, not purely on individual revenue, aligning partners to pass work to colleagues with better expertise or capacity rather than hoarding mandates. The firm targets being **top-3 in each practice area** rather than competing solely on overall size rankings.

## Work Processes & Methodology

### Client Service Model
IndusLaw's service model is built around **practice-group specialisation** rather than generalist coverage. Partners own client relationships and transaction mandates, with cross-selling encouraged through the democratic equity model (partners are not penalised for passing work to more appropriate colleagues).

### Transaction Execution
- Multi-disciplinary deal teams led by a partner with associate support at 2–4 layers (senior associate → associate → trainee)
- Strong emphasis on PE/VC and M&A transaction structuring, including offshore fund documentation and FEMA compliance
- Active in NCLT proceedings (CIRP, liquidation) following the IBC 2016 reforms

### Cross-Border Integration (post-CMS)
Following the May 2025 CMS alliance:
- Joint pitch capability with CMS lawyers in Europe, Middle East, Asia-Pacific
- Inbound India mandates handled by IndusLaw for CMS's 7,200+ global lawyer client base
- Outbound Indian client expansions supported by local CMS offices in 50 countries

### Billing
Standard hourly-rate and matter-fee billing typical of tier-1 Indian firms. Specific rates are not publicly disclosed; Glassdoor salary data indicates associate compensation of approximately ₹1.08–1.17 lakh/month, consistent with tier-1 Indian market rates.

## Client Complaints & Criticism

### Major Attrition Events (2024)

**August 2024 — Capital Markets Exodus:**
In India's largest group legal hire of 2024, **Manan Lahoty** (capital markets practice head, partner since 2019) departed to **Cyril Amarchand Mangaldas (CAM)** with:
- 8 partners: Abhyuday Bhotika, Ravi Dubey, Abhiroop Lahiri, Manshoor Nazki, Puneet Passi, Priyadarshini Rao, Anshul Roy, Vishal Yaduvanshi
- 40+ associates — a total of 50–60 lawyers representing the entirety of IndusLaw's capital markets team

This was described by legal media as "India's largest hire of the year" and represented a near-total loss of the firm's capital markets practice, which had itself been assembled via lateral recruitment (Lahoty had been poached from Luthra & Luthra in 2019).

**2024 — Delhi Disputes Departure:**
Partner **Padmaja Kaul** (disputes; formerly from Trilegal, joined 2019) moved to JSA with her team of 4 lawyers (July 2024).

**2024 — Infrastructure Departure:**
A 13-lawyer infrastructure group moved to JSA, led by partners M. Arun Kumar and Deepak Chowdhury.

**Estimated scale:** Approximately 22+ partners departed within a roughly 3-month window in mid-2024, representing approximately 30% of the partnership.

### IndusLaw's Differentiated Position

1. **CMS alliance (2025):** First among the top Indian full-service firms to join a major international network, providing potential inbound/outbound deal flow advantage over AZB, Trilegal, Khaitan
2. **NLSIU pedigree:** Strong NLS alumni network in founding team creates recruitment and referral advantages in Bengaluru
3. **Technology/VC/Startup focus:** Deep relationships with startup ecosystem — advises 50%+ of India's unicorns — a segment where CAM and Khaitan are less specialised
4. **Democratic equity structure:** Differentiates talent attraction pitch from hierarchical legacy firms
5. **Hyderabad strength:** Historically dominant in Hyderabad; IFLR Best Firm Hyderabad 3 consecutive years

### Market Context (Foreign Law Firm Entry)
The CMS alliance timing is strategic. Bar Council of India regulatory changes are expected to allow foreign law firms to establish full Indian presences. By joining CMS now as an independent Indian member firm, IndusLaw/CMS is positioning to capture inbound foreign-firm-referral work before competitors formalise international relationships. Founding partner Suneeth Katarki explicitly cited this context in announcing the alliance.
