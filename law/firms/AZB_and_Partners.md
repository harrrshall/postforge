### Brief History

AZB & Partners was formed in 2004 through the merger of two legacy practices:
- **CZB & Partners** (Mumbai) — the practice of Zia Mody and Bahram Vakil
- **Ajay Bahl & Company** (Delhi) — the practice of Ajay Bahl, formerly a Chartered Accountant who had interned with Attorney General Soli Sorabjee

The three founding partners brought distinct backgrounds: Zia Mody (Cambridge + Harvard, ex-Baker McKenzie New York), Bahram Vakil (Columbia, ex-Little & Co., US-qualified), and Ajay Bahl (CA + law, Delhi-anchored corporate practice). The merged firm rapidly became one of India's highest-ranked full-service corporate law firms. The firm is credited with professionalizing the Indian law firm partnership model, moving away from the traditional family-style set-up toward a more institutionalized structure with a clear partnership track.

In 2024, AZB was named "Best Overall Law Firm" at the India Business Law Journal Awards and topped the Venture Intelligence M&A and PE-VC league tables for legal advisors.

In September 2025, the firm announced the firm-wide adoption of Harvey, the legal generative AI platform, becoming one of the first major Indian law firms to deploy it at scale alongside Shardul Amarchand Mangaldas.

## Organizational Hierarchy

AZB & Partners operates a conventional magic-circle-style tiered hierarchy, which it has deliberately institutionalized away from the informal family-model common in Indian law firms. The career track is as follows:

```
Co-Founders / Managing Partners
        |
Senior Partners (Equity)
        |
Partners (Equity)
        |
Salaried / Nominal Partners
        |
Counsel / Senior Counsel
        |
Senior Associates (typically 5–8 years PQE)
        |
Associates (typically 1–4 years PQE)
        |
Trainee Associates / Law Clerks / Interns
```

**Governance:** The firm is co-led by Managing Partners Zia Mody (Mumbai) and Ajay Bahl (Delhi/NCR). Bahram Vakil holds Founding Partner status. Day-to-day management is distributed across practice group heads. The firm maintains a Managing Partner Committee that sets firm-wide strategy.

**Partnership Track:** Associates typically spend 6–9 years before being considered for Counsel or Partner elevation. The firm has been criticized (on Legally India forums) for slow partnership timelines and limited transparency in the elevation process.

**Support Functions:** The firm employs a dedicated Innovation Cell, a Business Development team, an IT/Technology division, HR, Finance, and Library/Knowledge Management teams.

## Work Processes & Methodology

### Client Service Model

AZB operates a **client-centric, team-led advisory model**. Clients are assigned dedicated teams comprising a Senior/Equity Partner (relationship owner and strategic lead), supporting Partners, Senior Associates, and Associates. The team composition scales with transaction complexity.

Key principles articulated by the firm:
- "Clients are at the centre of AZB's approach. Their needs shape the way the firm organises itself."
- "The firm seeks to work in partnership with its clients to anticipate and address the legal and regulatory aspects of their business goals and objectives."
- "The firm delves into every detail before offering strategic advice; in this, their execution is unparalleled." (client quote)

### Transaction Methodology (M&A/PE)

1. **Structuring & Pre-Deal Advisory** — Tax structuring, regulatory analysis, deal architecture
2. **Due Diligence** — Legal, regulatory, and title due diligence using custom software; structured reporting with risk matrices
3. **Negotiation & Drafting** — Term sheets, definitive agreements, ancillary documents
4. **Regulatory Filings** — CCI merger control, SEBI approvals, RBI/FEMA clearances, sectoral approvals
5. **Closing** — Use of proprietary Closing Tracker; interactive dashboards for client visibility
6. **Post-Closing** — Post-merger compliance, integration advisory

### Dispute Resolution Methodology

The disputes practice operates independently from the transactional side. AZB maintains a dedicated disputes team handling:
- Domestic litigation (High Courts, Supreme Court of India)
- International arbitration (ICC, SIAC, LCIA, DIAC, UNCITRAL ad hoc)
- Competition/antitrust investigations and appeals before CCI/NCLAT
- White-collar crime, regulatory enforcement, and investigations

### Business Development

Analytics and data from the firm's BI platform are used to generate customized pitch materials, experience statements, and partner profiles for BD purposes. The firm is a member of The World Law Group, which provides cross-referral networks for international matters.

## Client Complaints & Criticism

### Work Culture & Associate Experience

AZB has a significant volume of negative employee feedback published on Glassdoor (207+ reviews) and on Legally India's forums. The criticisms are recurrent and consistent:

**Glassdoor Ratings (as of early 2026):**
- Overall: 3.8 / 5
- Work-Life Balance: 2.6–3.0 / 5 (consistently low)
- Culture & Values: 3.2–3.3 / 5
- Career Opportunities: 4.0–4.3 / 5
- Compensation & Benefits: 3.9–4.4 / 5
- Recommend to a Friend: 78%

**Recurring Complaints:**

1. **Punishing hours:** Reviews consistently cite 12–14 hour days with weekend work expected. Associates report being "available 24/7" and pressure to drop personal commitments. Work-life balance is described as "non-existent" by multiple reviewers.

2. **Toxic elements in specific offices/teams:** The Mumbai disputes team and certain other teams have been the subject of extended forum discussions on Legally India (thread titles include "AZB resignation TOXIC," "AZB Mumbai Disputes Team – Need Advice," "Thoughts about AZB Mumbai Disputes team?"). Reviews describe partners putting "unrealistic expectations and inhumane deadlines."

3. **Bullpen layout enabling public criticism:** The open-plan layout at some offices means that negative partner feedback directed at associates occurs publicly, which multiple reviewers describe as humiliating.

4. **High attrition:** Legally India forum posts note that of cohorts joining together, zero members often remain after a few years. The attrition rate at AZB is considered among the highest for Indian Big Law firms.

5. **Opaque partnership track:** Associates report limited visibility into what is required for promotion to Partner. The absence of transparent criteria is a frequent grievance.

6. **Intern experience:** Interns report being given work without context, spending long hours in court without substantive involvement, and receiving information "in bits and parts."

7. **Competition team specific criticism:** One Legally India thread ("AZB Competition team – What's wrong") raised concerns about management style in that practice group specifically.

**Positive Aspects Noted by Employees:**
- Unmatched brand name and career launch value
- Quality and complexity of work (top-tier M&A, PE, and dispute mandates)
- Direct client interface for senior associates
- Strong compensation (₹15–25 lakh base for associates; ₹36 lakh+ for senior associates; ₹80L–1Cr for junior partners)
- Firm retreats (Goa and other destinations, twice annually)
- Strong learning environment for those who can sustain the pace

**Summary Assessment:** The trade-off at AZB is widely understood in the Indian legal market — the brand, deal quality, and compensation are top-tier, but the work culture and work-life balance are considered among the hardest in Indian Big Law. The firm's internal culture has improved incrementally (the Delhi office is generally considered better than Mumbai in this regard), but the core criticism of excessive hours and limited associate welfare remains.
