## Firm Overview

Phoenix Legal was established in 2008 by four co-founding partners — three former senior partners from Trilegal (Manjula Chawla, Saket Shukla, and Abhishek Saxena) and one from Kochhar & Co (Sawant Singh). The firm was built as an independent, mid-size full-service Indian law firm with a deliberate focus on high partner involvement and commercially pragmatic advice, positioning itself as a credible alternative to the "Big Six" Indian law firms.

The firm describes its differentiation as: partner-level involvement in every matter at all times, intensive legal research as a foundation for commercial problem-solving, and a lean partnership model that avoids the bureaucratic layers of larger firms.

Phoenix Legal advises a wide range of clients including domestic and multinational corporations, Fortune 100 and Fortune 500 companies, banks and financial institutions, private equity and venture capital funds, promoter groups, and public sector undertakings. The firm handles both purely domestic and complex cross-border matters.

Headline claim: In 2013, RSG Consulting rated Phoenix Legal as having the highest client satisfaction score (9.3/10) among India's top 20 law firms — a brand pillar the firm has continued to market.

Practice footprint: banking and finance, corporate/M&A, dispute resolution, projects and energy, restructuring and insolvency, shipping, antitrust and competition, private equity, real estate, employment, tax, IP, and TMT.

The firm publishes articles on Mondaq, Lexology, and its own website, and has been visible in the India Business Law Journal as a go-to comment source for banking, energy, insolvency, and insurance regulation.

## Organizational Hierarchy

Phoenix Legal operates as a traditional partnership model without a corporate holding structure. The hierarchy is:

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Co-Founding Partners** | 4 senior equity partners who founded the firm; retain management control |
| **Partners** | Equity and salaried partners; lead practice groups and client relationships |
| **Associate Partners** | Senior lawyers on a track to full partnership; client-facing but below full equity |
| **Senior Associates** | Mid-level fee earners, typically 5–8 years PQE |
| **Associates** | Junior fee earners; fresh law graduates and 1–4 year PQE lawyers |
| **Interns / Trainees** | Law students on 4-week structured internships |

The firm has no separate "Counsel" or "Of Counsel" designation in its public materials. Management decisions are concentrated among the co-founding partners and the broader equity partnership.

## Work Processes & Methodology

Phoenix Legal's differentiated methodology, as communicated in its own materials and rankings commentary, includes:

**1. High Partner Involvement**
The firm explicitly markets "partner-level involvement in all deals/transactions, at all times" as its core differentiator from larger firms. This means senior partners are expected to be directly accessible and hands-on rather than delegating to associates.

**2. Research-Intensive Approach**
Complex legal positions are validated through intensive, detailed legal research rather than relying on templated precedents. This is reflected in the firm's internship program, which emphasizes research and drafting as core skills developed from the beginning.

**3. Commercial Pragmatism**
Phoenix Legal positions itself as providing "commercially pragmatic, solution-oriented legal advice." Partners are expected to understand client business models to deliver advice that is practical rather than purely legally conservative.

**4. Sector Depth**
The firm organises practice groups by sector expertise, not just legal discipline. Partners like Pranav Srivastava (TMT + disputes), Pallavi Bedi (energy + project finance), and Piyush Mishra (insolvency + aviation finance) represent cross-disciplinary sector specialists rather than pure generalists.

**5. Transaction Management**
For large finance and M&A transactions, Phoenix Legal uses a deal-room model with dedicated transaction teams assembled around the lead partner. The January 2024 Sumit Sinha hire (with two colleagues joining simultaneously) reflects the firm's practice of onboarding lateral partners with their existing teams.

**6. Dispute Strategy**
The disputes team combines Supreme Court and High Court trial advocacy with arbitration (domestic and international), maintaining a model where disputes partners handle both litigated cases and transactional risk advisory.

### Competitive Dynamics
Phoenix Legal is consistently competitive for mid-to-large banking/finance mandates in India's project and infrastructure sector.
