## Firm Overview

**Khaitan & Co** is one of India's oldest and largest full-service law firms, founded on **November 11, 1911**, by **Debi Prasad Khaitan** in Kolkata. With over 113 years of continuous practice, it is widely regarded as a "heritage law firm" and holds the distinction of being the first Indian law firm to reach the 200-partner milestone (achieved in 2022).

The firm operates as a **Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)** and has grown from a single Kolkata office into a pan-India institution serving leading domestic conglomerates, multinational corporations, private equity funds, global investors, financial institutions, and government bodies.

## Organizational Hierarchy

Khaitan & Co operates a structured multi-tier hierarchy typical of large Indian law firms, with distinct equity and non-equity tracks.

### Hierarchy Levels (Senior to Junior)

1. **Senior Partner** — Top of the partnership. Haigreve Khaitan holds this sole designation.
2. **Equity Partner / Designated Partner** — Full equity stake; elected to governance roles. ~150+ equity partners.
3. **Non-Equity Partner / Salaried Partner** — Introduced after 2010 when the firm ended its all-equity partnership model.
4. **Principal Associate (PA) / Managing Associate** — Typically 6+ years post-qualification experience (PQE). Acts as matter lead.
5. **Senior Associate** — 3–5 years PQE.
6. **Associate** — Entry level through ~3 years PQE.
7. **Counsel / Of-Counsel** — Specialist non-partner track; often senior specialists or returning practitioners.
8. **LexFlex Consultant** — Flexible engagement contract professionals (see Technology & Tools section).

**Nature:** Proprietary flexible resourcing model / gig-legal platform  
**Launched:** c. 2021  
**Eligibility:** Lawyers with 2+ years PQE, legal tech professionals, knowledge management specialists  
**Model:**
- Non-exclusive when not on assignment
- Exclusive during active engagement (conflict management)
- Arrangements include: part-time hourly, specific days, period-based, or project-based
- Fully remote-capable or office access available
- Current Khaitan employees may join LexFlex for alternative arrangements

**Significance:** LexFlex was a pioneering model in the Indian legal market, allowing senior and retired lawyers to remain engaged with top-tier work on flexible terms. The platform gives the firm surge capacity without permanent headcount expansion.

## Work Processes & Methodology

### Matter Staffing: Matrix Model

Khaitan & Co uses a **matrix project-staffing structure**. Each matter is assembled with:
- A **partner** (relationship owner and lead advisor)
- **Principal Associates (PA)** acting as matter leads
- **Associates** for research and drafting
- **Counsel / specialists** as needed

This allows cross-practice team assembly for complex transactions (e.g., an M&A deal may draw from Corporate, Tax, Competition, Employment, and Real Estate teams simultaneously).

### Client Service Approach

The firm describes its philosophy as "responsive and relationship-driven." Key traits:
- **Sector-specific knowledge** — lawyers aligned to industry verticals
- **Jurisdictional expertise** — deep regulatory knowledge of each state/city office
- **Integrated ESG advisory** — cross-practice teams for environmental, social, governance matters
- **Thought Leadership** — KCO Compass blog, client alerts, regulatory updates, podcast

### Quality Control

- **Style guides** and documentation standards are part of the formal onboarding curriculum
- Partners typically review all client-facing work
- Known for rigorous due diligence processes in M&A and PE transactions

### Client Origination & Business Development

The firm operates a structured BD function. Senior partners like Haigreve Khaitan serve on corporate boards, generating referral and repeat business. The firm has a strong alumni network and active conference/publication presence.

### Working Hours / Culture

Based on Glassdoor and Legally India data:
- Hours are demanding, especially in transactional practices (Corporate, PE, M&A)
- Work spikes around deal closings and regulatory deadlines
- "Long hours and unpredictable client meetings" is a frequently cited challenge
- PA-level and above carry significant independent responsibility

## Client Complaints & Criticism

### 1. Sexual Harassment (POSH) Controversy — 2019

The most publicized controversy at the firm involved a **former female associate's resignation** after alleging the POSH (Prevention of Sexual Harassment) internal committee mishandled her complaint.

**Key allegations in the 12-page resignation letter (addressed to Haigreve Khaitan):**
- The POSH committee found the inappropriate conduct occurred but ruled the accused "did not intend to sexually harass" — the complainant argued intent is not a legal requirement under the POSH Act
- Meetings scheduled with only 10–17 minutes' notice instead of the required 5-day notice
- The accused's written statement was forwarded only 10 minutes before meetings
- Only the accused was asked to submit a witness list; the complainant was not given equal opportunity
- A partner allegedly disclosed personal details shared in confidence to the POSH committee
- The complainant was never asked for her written statement until after the accused had submitted his

**Firm response:** Khaitan's HR executive stated the firm has "all required processes, checks & balances in place" but declined detailed comment citing confidentiality. The response was widely criticized for being inadequate and formulaic.

**Follow-up:** Bar & Bench and Legally India reported the incident received significant legal community attention. Commenters noted allegations of recurring cultural issues.

### 2. Work-Life Balance

Employee reviews on Glassdoor and Legally India consistently flag:
- Extremely long and unpredictable hours, especially in transactional practices
- "No work life balance" cited as the primary con in multiple reviews
- "Too much power in the hands of the partners" — governance opacity concern
- "Office politics" in some offices

Glassdoor rating for **work-life balance: 3.1/5** (notably lower than overall rating of 3.9/5).

### 3. Partnership Structure Change — 2010 Controversy

When Khaitan & Co ended its **all-equity partnership** model around 2010 to introduce salaried/associate partners, the move generated industry debate. Legally India reported this as an "unprecedented step" — the firm had previously operated as a pure equity partnership for nearly a century. The transition created uncertainty about internal promotion pathways for associates.

### 4. Partner Departures

Notable exits include:
- **Sachin Mandlik** (Dispute Resolution Partner) — left to go independent; firm publicly committed to "ramping up litigation" after his departure
- **Abhishek Sinha** (Equity Partner) — joined Bar & Brief Attorneys for its corporate practice
- Various senior laterals have moved to competing firms over the years

### 5. General Cultural Observations (Glassdoor)

- Some reviewers note that **Bengaluru office** is rated higher (4.4/5) than the firm average, suggesting uneven culture across offices
- **Mumbai** rates 3.8/5 — broadly positive but with the standard long-hours caveats
- Newer entrants cite a steep learning curve with limited structured mentorship in some practice groups
