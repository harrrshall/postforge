## Firm Overview

S&R Associates is a full-service Indian law firm with a strong focus on transactional and capital markets work. Founded in 2005, the firm has built a reputation as one of India's leading boutique-elite practices for cross-border M&A, equity and debt capital markets, and private equity transactions. The firm currently has approximately **160 lawyers**, including **40 partners and counsel**, operating from two offices in New Delhi and Mumbai.

The firm positions itself on three pillars: **quality of advice**, **hands-on partner involvement at every stage of a transaction**, and an **inter-disciplinary approach** that draws on deep regulatory expertise across practice groups. This partner-intensive model distinguishes S&R from larger full-service firms that rely heavily on associate teams.

S&R's lawyers are admitted to practice in India, and many have prior experience in major international jurisdictions including the United States, United Kingdom, and Singapore. The founding partner, Sandip Bhagat, previously worked at Shearman & Sterling in New York and Singapore, and this international pedigree pervades the firm's DNA. The firm explicitly aims to deliver "international quality legal services" with Indian law expertise.

The firm serves a sophisticated domestic and international client base including global investment banks, PE funds, multinational corporations, and large Indian conglomerates. It is regularly instructed on landmark transactions in the Indian capital markets and M&A space.

## Organizational Hierarchy

S&R Associates follows a tiered structure typical of elite Indian law firms, broadly:

```
Founding/Senior Partners
        |
    Partners
        |
  Retained Partners (intermediate designation, post-2025)
        |
    Counsel / Senior Associates
        |
    Associates (A0 / A1 / A2 / A3 / Senior)
        |
    Trainees / Interns
```

**Key structural notes:**
- **Partners:** Equity and non-equity partnership; the firm uses a "retained partner" designation (introduced formally in 2025) for senior associates being elevated into a partnership track without full equity
- **Counsel:** Senior lawyers with specialised expertise who may not be on the equity track; the firm currently has around 40 in the combined partner + counsel layer
- **Associates:** Graded by seniority (A0 = fresher; A1, A2, A3 = progressively senior); total associate headcount estimated at ~120
- **No "Of Counsel" designation commonly used** — the firm prefers the counsel or partner label

Partner promotions are competitive and relatively infrequent, reflecting the firm's controlled growth philosophy. The October 2025 retained partner designations (Bhargava, Khanna, Sharma) were notable precisely because they were the first cohort under a formalised intermediate track.

## Work Processes & Methodology

### Transaction Execution Model
- **High partner-to-associate ratio involvement:** Unlike some larger Indian firms where associates handle much of the drafting independently, S&R's model emphasises partner oversight at each transaction stage. This is a core differentiator cited in Chambers and Legal 500 commentary.
- **Inter-disciplinary task forces:** Complex deals routinely involve multiple practice groups working simultaneously (e.g., M&A + Tax + Regulatory + Competition on a large acquisition). The firm's size allows integration without bureaucratic silos.
- **International standards framing:** Many mandates are structured to meet global institutional investor requirements, reflecting the firm's international client base. Partners with US/UK experience calibrate documentation standards accordingly.

### Capital Markets Workflow
- Issuer-side mandates: prospectus drafting, due diligence coordination, SEBI filings, stock exchange compliance
- Underwriter-side mandates: comfort letter, legal opinions, representation and warranty review
- Post-listing regulatory compliance advisory
- REIT/InvIT structuring: complex multi-jurisdictional structures involving offshore holding companies, trust law, SEBI InvIT/REIT regulations

### M&A Workflow
- Full-cycle transaction management: term sheet, due diligence, share purchase / business transfer agreements, regulatory approvals (CCI, FIPB/FEMA, sector regulators), post-closing integration
- Strong cross-border capability with understanding of both Indian law and the commercial expectations of international counterparties (US, European, Singapore-based PE funds and corporates)

### Dispute Resolution Workflow
- Niti Dixit-led practice handles Supreme Court and High Court commercial litigation alongside international arbitration
- Shahezad Kazi (Mumbai) handles commercial and real estate litigation and insolvency
- BIT claim capability is rare among Indian law firms and positions S&R well for investor-state disputes

### Client Communication
- Regular publications and client alerts across all major practice areas (hosted at snrlaw.in/publications/)
- Active thought leadership on Lexology, Mondaq, and Legal 500's news platform

## Client Complaints & Criticism

The following reflects publicly available commentary, primarily from anonymous forums (Legally India) and aggregated review platforms. All anonymous commentary is unverified.

### Recurring Themes in Criticism (Legally India forums)

**1. Career progression opacity**
The most consistent complaint across multiple Legally India threads is the absence of a transparent or articulated career path. Associates report:
- No structured performance review conversations
- Advancement depends on informal partner relationships ("favouritism") rather than published criteria
- The firm "fails to make space for people at the top" due to limited partnership slots at the senior end
- High attrition of capable mid-level lawyers who leave out of frustration: "most deserving people end up leaving"

**2. Cultural coldness / transactional environment**
Multiple reviewers describe the firm atmosphere as "frigid," "cold," or "overly transactional." Specific criticisms:
- Management keeps "cold behaviour" with staff, preventing loyalty or belonging
- Interpersonal insecurity is pervasive; associates are reluctant to challenge partners or offer original thinking
- One ex-employee described it as a "control freak firm with no transparency"
- The instruction to "just do what partners tell you without presenting ideas" is cited as stifling

**3. Alleged mass layoffs (2022–2023 threads)**
One Legally India thread alleged "at least six mid-level resources have been asked to leave" in a round of departures attributed to overhiring relative to available work. The allegation was directly refuted by a self-identified S&R associate who called it "clearly an attempt to push an agenda." Partner Shivaji Bhattacharya reportedly resigned to join a private bank during this period; one associate (Rachita) departed for Trilegal. The veracity of a coordinated "mass firing" is disputed.

**4. Mid/senior-level compensation**
While junior associate pay is praised (see below), there are complaints that mid-to-senior compensation does not keep pace with market benchmarks: "not at par with the market at mid/senior level lawyers." This creates a retention problem at the 4–7 year level.

**5. Intern treatment**
Some interns and junior associates have noted that certain associates' behaviour lacks empathy and can be difficult. However, internship reviews also include strongly positive experiences.

### Positive Counterpoints (from same sources)

- S&R is broadly recognised as offering **the highest or among the highest junior associate salaries** in the Indian law firm market. One Legally India post cites "19.8 [lakh per annum] for an A0" — a figure that, if accurate, would represent the top of the Indian market
- The firm is described as "the gold standard in capital markets (both debt and equity) as far as deal-making and execution"
- Glassdoor aggregate rating: **4.0 out of 5** (overall); work-life balance 3.5/5; culture & values 3.8/5; career opportunities 4.0/5; compensation & benefits 3.9/5; 69% of reviewers would recommend the firm to a friend
- Work quality and exposure to landmark transactions are consistently praised

### Structural / Market Criticism

- The firm's controlled-growth strategy means it periodically tightens hiring relative to deal flow, creating boom-bust cycles in associate headcount that can feel destabilising
- Its boutique-elite positioning, while a strength for quality, means clients requiring a broader geographic footprint (Bengaluru, Chennai, Hyderabad) or full-service regulatory depth must supplement with other firms
