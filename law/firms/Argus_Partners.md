## Firm Overview

Argus Partners is a mid-tier Indian full-service business law firm with pan-India offices in Mumbai, Delhi, Bengaluru, and Kolkata. It positions itself as a firm staffed by "business lawyers" who understand commercial and financial issues and can deliver practical, risk-calibrated legal solutions — a deliberate distinction from purely academic or court-focused practice.

### Founding & Corporate History

- **2009**: Krishnava Dutt founded Argus Partners after leaving Amarchand Mangaldas (where he had been made Partner in 2007). The firm started in a 300 sq ft office on Loudon Street, Kolkata.
- **2009–2010**: Within its first year, the firm expanded to Mumbai.
- **2012**: Argus Partners merged with the Mumbai-headquartered firm **Udwadia & Udeshi** (est. 1997), creating the merged entity **Udwadia Udeshi & Argus Partners**.
- **2015**: The firm rebranded to simply **Argus Partners** and formally adopted its current core values — Integrity, Quality, Respect.
- **2022**: Hired five partners (Abhinav Bhalaik, Ankit Guha, Armaan Patkar, Namitha Mathews, Neha Madan) from Algo Legal along with teams of ~35 lawyers, significantly bolstering Corporate/M&A, PE/VC, and TMT capacity across Mumbai, Delhi, and Bengaluru.
- **2024 (October)**: Added a 10-member corporate and M&A team from IndusLaw to the Bengaluru office, led by equity partner Anindya Ghosh and partners Ashwin Krishnan and Anantha Krishnan Iyer; concurrently relocated the Bengaluru office to larger premises at SKAV 909, Lavelle Road.
- **2024–2025**: Multiple new partners hired across Banking & Finance (Aseem Dhawan, Delhi), PE/VC (Pallavi Kanakagiri, Bengaluru), Energy & Infrastructure (Ankur Arora, Delhi), and Regulatory Disputes (Mazag Andrabi, Delhi).
- **March 2026**: Promoted Soorjya Ganguli to Senior Partner; elevated Jitendra Soni to Equity Partner; promoted Jaidrath Zaveri to Salaried Partner.

## Work Processes & Methodology

### Transaction Execution
- **Multi-disciplinary deal teams**: M&A transactions are staffed with lawyers from corporate, tax, disputes, regulatory, and antitrust teams simultaneously — not sequential handoffs.
- **End-to-end ownership**: Each matter is managed from structuring through due diligence, documentation, regulatory clearances, and post-closing advisory.
- **Client-as-only-client philosophy**: The firm espouses treating each client as the only client — understanding their business aspirations, constraints, and risk tolerance to structure bespoke solutions.
- **Practical risk identification**: Emphasis on surfacing commercial (not merely legal) risks; partners are trained to assess financial and accounting dimensions of transactions.

### Dispute Representation
- Regular NCLT/NCLAT appearances for insolvency and media litigation matters
- Supreme Court and High Court representation through senior counsel empanelments
- Separate teams for commercial arbitration and domestic court litigation

### Knowledge Dissemination
- **Daily KM updates**: Internal bulletins on legal developments circulated across all offices
- **IBC Fortnightly Summary**: Biweekly publication covering all NCLAT orders; publicly available on the firm's website and widely followed by insolvency practitioners
- **Tech Newsletter**: Monthly publication on technology law, data privacy, and emerging tech issues
- **Argus Talks podcast**: Regular audio episodes on practice-specific legal topics

### Hiring & Staffing
- Campus recruitment from NLU-tier law schools (NALSAR, NUJS, NLU Delhi are specifically noted in online discussions)
- Lateral partner hiring has been a significant growth driver (Algo Legal 2022; IndusLaw 2024)
- Fresher intake: 21 freshers from 8 law schools in the most recent campus cycle (Bar & Bench coverage); starting salary ~₹16 LPA
