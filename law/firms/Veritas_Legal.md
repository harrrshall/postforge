### Firm Philosophy

Veritas Legal was deliberately built around the principle of "depth as opposed to breadth." Abhijit Joshi founded the firm after leaving AZB & Partners — where he had been Senior Partner and CEO for 13+ years — with the explicit goal of creating a boutique that could outperform larger firms on complex, senior-staffed matters. He deliberately avoided naming the firm after himself to ensure institutional goodwill and succession continuity.

Core values as stated by leadership: **transparency, communication, collaborative participation, meritocracy.** The firm's tagline is to offer "a somewhat different and refreshing alternative" in India's legal market.

Within its first few years, Veritas was recognized by RSG in partnership with the Financial Times as the **"Fastest Rising Boutique Law Firm"** in India.

## Organizational Hierarchy

The firm operates as an **equity partnership** with a profit-sharing model. Partners share profits based on equity percentage — described by Joshi as "you sink or sail with the firm." The organizational structure is:

```
Founding & Managing Partner (Abhijit Joshi)
    |
Senior Partners (5: Rustam Mulla, Kunal Doshi, Tushar B. Raut, Manav Raheja, Udayan Choksi)

           Sukhada Wagle)
    |

    |
```

The firm employs over 50 lawyers in total. The partner-to-associate ratio is kept intentionally lean to ensure senior involvement on all matters — a key differentiator emphasized in all firm communications.

## Practice Areas

Veritas Legal operates across 13 practice areas, grouped into three broad clusters:

### Cluster 1: Transactional / Corporate

1. **Mergers & Acquisitions** — Core practice. Covers acquisitions, divestitures, cross-border deals, restructurings, joint ventures, and public M&A. Recognized in Band 3 (Chambers Global/Asia-Pacific) and Tier 3 (Legal 500). Notable clients: TVS Group, FedEx, Aegis Logistics.

2. **Private Equity & Venture Capital** — Core practice alongside M&A. Represents domestic and international PE/VC firms at every investment stage. Sector focus: pharmaceuticals, fintech, real estate, e-commerce. IFLR1000 Tier 2. Notable clients/deals: TPG (Dr. Agarwal's Healthcare — INR 1,050 crore, largest Indian eye care fundraise); Partners Group (Ecom Express — $250M+); Creador PE (Hinduja Tech — $50M / INR 416 crore).

3. **Private Equity in Real Estate (PERE)** — Integrated offering combining PE investment structuring with real estate title, regulatory compliance, and dispute resolution. Handles structuring, documentation, title evaluation.

4. **Real Estate** — Full lifecycle coverage from acquisition through development, leasing, and financing. Notable deals: K. Raheja–GIC (Worli acquisition), Leela Palace hotel assets sale to Brookfield.

5. **Banking & Finance** — Securitization, syndicated lending, ECBs, debt restructuring, fintech. Led by Jhinook Roy.

6. **Projects & Infrastructure** — Greenfield/brownfield projects, PPPs, financing across energy, ports, airports, roads, mining. Multi-sector coverage; led by Jhinook Roy.

7. **Restructuring & Insolvency** — Advises banks, multinationals, and NBFCs on insolvency planning, corporate restructuring, and distressed asset recovery.

8. **Labour & Employment** — Employment contracts, non-compete enforcement, ESOP structuring, labour disputes. Serves investment banking, PE, technology, and education sectors.

### Cluster 2: Disputes & Regulatory

9. **Litigation & Dispute Resolution** — Civil and criminal matters in Supreme Court, High Courts, NCLT, and SEBI. Institutional and ad hoc arbitration. Notable clients: Central Depository Services (CDSL), Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE).

10. **White-Collar Crime** — Economic offenses, fraud, anti-corruption, money laundering, FCPA matters. Represents individuals and multinationals. Notable representations: ICICI Bank and Deloitte (IL&FS-related matters).

11. **Competition / Antitrust** — Merger control (CCI clearances), anti-competitive agreements, abuse of dominant position, licensing arrangements. Sectors: IT, pharma, telecoms. Notable: Zepto minority investment notification analysis; FedEx–Delhivery CCI approval.

### Cluster 3: Advisory / Specialized

12. **Private Client** — Cross-border wealth planning, generational succession, estate planning for resident and NRI HNWIs with global assets. Chambers Global Band 2 (High Net Worth). Notable clients: Y.K. Hamied (Cipla non-executive chairman), Anupam Mittal (People Group / Shark Tank India judge), Vageesh Gupta (MD, Partners Group India).

## Work Processes & Methodology

### Matter Staffing Philosophy

The firm's core differentiator is **senior staffing on every matter.** Abhijit Joshi has repeatedly stated in interviews that in approximately 75% of Veritas Legal matters, the opposing counsel represented a larger firm — yet Veritas wins or holds its own due to depth of partner-level involvement rather than large associate teams.

- Senior partners are directly involved in drafting, negotiation, and client communication — not delegated away to juniors
- Boutique model allows faster turnaround than larger firms due to shorter decision chains
- The firm positions itself as "solution-oriented" and emphasizes commercial, practical legal thinking over purely academic legal analysis

### Client Service Model

- Personalized, hands-on service
- Emphasis on 360-degree problem analysis (holistic view of legal risk across practice areas)
- Transparent communication with clients through the matter lifecycle
- Pro bono culture is integrated: over **3,000 hours per year** contributed voluntarily by lawyers, conducted during office hours using firm resources

### Deal Process

For M&A and PE transactions:
1. Conflict check and client onboarding
2. Due diligence (legal, regulatory, sector-specific including CCI merger filings if required)
3. Transaction structuring and documentation
4. Negotiation with counterparty counsel
5. Regulatory approvals (CCI, SEBI, RBI, FIPB/DPIIT as applicable)
6. Closing and post-closing

The firm has demonstrated capability in obtaining CCI approvals (FedEx-Delhivery being a documented example) alongside deal execution.

## Client Complaints & Criticism

### Internal / Employee Complaints (Glassdoor, Legally India)

**Negative themes identified across anonymous review platforms:**

1. **Work hours / Work-life balance:** Multiple anonymous reviews cite extreme hours — "working every day more than 12–15 hours, no sick leaves, 7 days working, working till 3–5 am as a norm." This is consistent with the broader culture of elite Indian law firms but has been specifically noted for Veritas Legal.

2. **Management behavior:** Isolated reports of a senior partner being "very rude" to staff. Interns reported instances of "people screaming/yelling at interns" and "disorganized work allocation" in one Lawctopus review (this appears to be from an early-period intern).

3. **HR practices:** An anonymous Legally India commenter described HR as "impatient, immature, condescending, and passive aggressive" with references to inappropriate comments about employees' physical appearance. This was flagged as an unverified complaint.

4. **Bonus discrepancy:** Allegations of "promising higher bonuses but delivering much lower bonus at year-end." Unverified.

5. **Operational minutiae:** One reviewer noted the requirement to maintain a "printer master sheet to keep track of what prints" — cited as an example of micro-management.

6. **Partner departures:** Key partner Rahul Dwarkadas's departure in 2023 was noted by some commenters on Legally India as affecting firm stability and client retention in the disputes practice.

**Positive themes:**
- Good overall work culture cited by multiple reviewers
- Strong mentorship from senior partners
- High quality of work and client exposure
- Competitive compensation following the 40% pay hike
- Pro bono program well-regarded by lawyers who participated

**Overall assessment:** The complaints are broadly consistent with what is reported for elite Indian boutique and Tier 1 firms. The extreme hours issue is endemic to transactional Indian law practice. The HR behavioral complaints are more specific and notable, but unverified. No regulatory complaints, bar complaints, or client-side formal grievances have been identified in public sources.

### Market / Client-Side Criticism

No documented client complaints, regulatory sanctions, or public controversy found in Bar & Bench, LiveLaw, or court records searched. The firm's position in competitive transactions (where opposing counsel is often from Tier 1 firms) has not generated any documented client dissatisfaction in public reporting.

Veritas Legal is positioned as a **premium boutique** that punches above its size in transactional complexity. Its competitive advantages:

1. **Senior partner access** — Joshi and senior partners are personally involved in deals, unlike Tier 1 firms where senior partners may delegate execution to teams
2. **Pharma/healthcare sector depth** — Tushar Raut and Natasha Sethna have built a recognized niche in India's complex pharma M&A environment
3. **PE/VC network** — Consistent top-20 performance in Venture Intelligence tables despite having far fewer lawyers than Tier 1 firms
4. **Private wealth** — Chambers Tier 2 in Private Client (High Net Worth) is a strong differentiator; few Indian firms have robust HNI wealth structuring practices
5. **Alumni advantage** — Abhijit Joshi's AZB background and relationships from 13+ years there provide deal flow from relationships built over decades

**Vulnerabilities:**
- Single Mumbai office limits geographic reach; clients with pan-India litigation or deals in Delhi/Bengaluru may prefer firms with local presence
- Departure of key laterals (Rahul Dwarkadas in disputes) created practice gaps that took time to fill (Vaibhav Singh from SAM was brought in to address this)
- At ~98 professionals, the firm is capacity-constrained for mega-deals requiring large due diligence teams
- Partnership is concentrated among a cohort of former AZB/JSA/SAM alumni — potential for further departures if culture or compensation diverges from market
