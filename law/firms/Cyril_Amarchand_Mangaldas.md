**Background:** CAM was formed in May 2015 following the dissolution of the historic Amarchand & Mangaldas & Suresh A Shroff & Co. partnership, which was triggered by a succession dispute between brothers Cyril Shroff and Shardul Shroff. The firm retained the larger share of the predecessor firm's assets, talent pool, and clients. Despite the split, CAM positioned itself as the market leader in India, consistently ranked Band 1 / Tier 1 across all major legal directories.

## Organizational Hierarchy

CAM operates a tiered partnership structure typical of large Indian law firms:

```
Managing Partner
    └── Senior Management / Advisory Board
          └── Senior Partners (Practice Leaders)
                └── Partners (Equity Partners)
                      └── Partners (Salaried / Non-Equity Partners)
                            └── Counsel / Of Counsel
                                  └── Senior Associates
                                        └── Associates
                                              └── Junior Associates / Trainees / Interns
```

**Business Services Layer (parallel to legal hierarchy):**
- Chief Knowledge Officer (CKO)
- Chief Technology Officer / Head of LegalTech
- Management Information Systems & Billing Head
- Human Resources Director
- Business Development / Marketing Leadership

**Key structural notes:**
- The firm uses a lockstep-influenced equity model but has moved toward merit-based elements
- Practice groups are organized by both subject matter (e.g., Capital Markets, Banking & Finance) and industry verticals (e.g., Healthcare, Infrastructure)
- Each major office has a designated Office Head / Senior Partner anchor
- The Prarambh LegalTech Incubator functions as a standalone innovation arm
- A dedicated Knowledge & Learning team operates independently of billing practice groups

## Work Processes & Methodology

### Matter Lifecycle
1. **Client Onboarding & Conflict Check** — conducted via ERP and DMS conflict-checking tools before matter acceptance
2. **Matter Opening** — assigned matter number, team staffing by practice group heads
3. **Research & Analysis** — AI-assisted legal research tools + traditional databases (SCC Online, Manupatra, Westlaw India)
4. **Drafting** — document automation templates; AI-generated first drafts reviewed by associates/partners
5. **Review & Quality Control** — senior associate / partner review cycle; proofreading tools for consistency
6. **Client Communication** — deal rooms via iManage Share; structured update cycles
7. **Billing** — managed by dedicated MIS & Billing team; primarily hourly billing model with some fixed-fee mandates for larger clients
8. **Matter Closure & Knowledge Capture** — deal documents filed to DMS; precedents updated in knowledge repository

### Transaction Work (M&A / Capital Markets)
- Multi-disciplinary team deployment — corporate + tax + regulatory + employment partners assigned to large deals
- Dedicated transaction management platform integrated with DMS
- Use of virtual data rooms (VDRs) for due diligence
- Co-authoring capabilities for simultaneous multi-party document drafting

### Dispute Resolution Methodology
- End-to-end case management platform for litigation
- Technology-Assisted Review (TAR) for high-volume document discovery
- Arbitration teams routinely advise on SIAC, ICC, LCIA, and ad hoc India-seated arbitrations
- Cyril Shroff personally sits on the SIAC Board

### Design Thinking
- Associates trained in problem-solving via design thinking methodology — solution-first framing for complex mandates

## Client Complaints & Criticism

### Employee Reviews (Glassdoor / Indeed)

**Overall Glassdoor Rating:** 3.7 / 5.0 (based on ~99 anonymous reviews)

**Recommendation Rate:** 67% of employees would recommend working at CAM to a friend

#### Common Positives
- High-quality, landmark transactional and litigation work
- Strong brand name and client roster
- Good mentorship from senior lawyers in select teams
- Competitive pay relative to Indian law firm market
- Excellent training for freshers in fundamentals of corporate law
- Immaculate induction and training program for new joiners
- Good exposure to complex, high-value matters early in career

#### Recurring Complaints

| Issue | Frequency / Notes |
|---|---|
| Work-life balance | Most common complaint — "Very hectic and no work-life balance" (28+ reviews) |
| Bad work culture | 11+ reviews specifically cite "bad work culture" |
| Long hours | Routinely cited; expectation of availability on weekends and evenings |
| Hierarchical environment | Extremely formal; ideas require lengthy clearance chains; difficult to innovate from the bottom |
| Leave policy | Only 29 leaves per year (including sick leave); timesheet rules reportedly deduct leaves and impose financial penalties for shortfalls |
| Exploitative management | Cited in multiple reviews; pressure culture in certain practice groups |
| Toxic teams | Team-specific toxicity reported; Glassdoor advice to "pick teams carefully" |
| Slow career progression | Partner track is very long and competitive; many senior associates leave before making partner |
| Limited flexibility | Rigid policies on WFH/hybrid post-pandemic; stricter than peer firms |

#### Specific Reported Issues
- Timesheet penalties: Alleged financial deductions from salary for failing to meet minimum billable hour requirements — considered punitive by reviewers
- Leave counting: Sick leaves included in the total 29-day leave entitlement — rated lower than competitor firms
- Office culture described as "regressive" by some Bengaluru and Mumbai-based reviewers
- Junior lawyers report difficulty getting credit for contributions on large transactions
