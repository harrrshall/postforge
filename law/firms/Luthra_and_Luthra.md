### Summary Description
Luthra and Luthra Law Offices India is a Tier-1 Indian law firm founded in 1990 to coincide with India's economic liberalization. It is recognized as a pioneer in commercial legal advice and has grown from a single-person operation into one of India's leading full-service law firms. The firm advises some of the largest Indian and multinational corporations across 18+ practice areas and has facilitated some of India's landmark and first-of-kind transactions in project finance, competition law, restructuring, and M&A.

The firm was built on "integrity" as its cornerstone value and is known for "seamlessly executing high-value, complex transactions" across multiple sectors and jurisdictions. Several of its current senior partners are homegrown, reflecting a strong internal development culture — though the firm has also been significantly reshaped by departures and mergers since 2021.

## Client Complaints & Criticism

### The Mohit Saraf Dispute (2021) — Founding-Level Schism

This is the most significant adverse event in the firm's history. Mohit Saraf was the **co-founder** of the firm alongside Rajiv Luthra, and the two ran L&L Partners as a two-person partnership. Saraf was credited with building the firm from a handful of lawyers into a 350-lawyer enterprise over two decades, with particular strength in corporate and M&A work from the Mumbai office.

**Dispute Details:**
- Differences arose over **sharing of firm ownership with younger partners** — Saraf reportedly pushed for broader partnership equity distribution; Luthra resisted
- The dispute escalated into litigation before the **Delhi High Court**
- Rajiv Luthra was accused by Saraf's camp of **"illegally expelling" Saraf from the partnership**
- In **June 2021**, the parties reached an **interim settlement**: Saraf departed on receipt of approximately **USD 7 million** (roughly INR 52 crore)
- Media described the dispute as a "bitter leadership dispute" (Global Legal Post, July 2021)

**The Breakaway:**
- Saraf departed with **21 partners** and a team of approximately 100 lawyers
- The breakaway firm, **Saraf and Partners**, launched on 19 July 2021
- Notable early recruits included corporate stars from L&L: Jhawar, Kakkar, and others
- Saraf and Partners opened offices in Delhi, Mumbai, and Bengaluru from day one
- This represented the single largest partner exodus from any Indian law firm at the time

**Impact on Luthra:**
- The firm was temporarily destabilized, losing significant M&A, PE, and corporate talent concentrated in Mumbai
- Rankings slipped in certain practice areas between 2021–2023
- Firm was described in a 2022 Law.com article as "staging a comeback"

### Subsequent Partner Attrition (2025)
The year 2025 saw a significant wave of high-profile departures:

1. **Karan Mitroo** (Banking & Finance, Legal 500 Leading Partner) — departed to **JSA Advocates & Solicitors** in March 2025 with his team of 15 attorneys including 3 partners (Purvi Dabbiru, Kartikeya Dubey, Ankita Parasar). Mitroo had been at Luthra since 2008.

2. **Nishant Singh** (Corporate/M&A, Legal 500 Leading Partner, Banking & Finance) — departed to **Shardul Amarchand Mangaldas** in April 2025 with a team of 12 lawyers from the Mumbai office. Singh cited the need for "deeper support from competition law, tax, disputes and other specialist practices."

3. **Vaibhav Suri** (Real Estate) — departed to **Cyril Amarchand Mangaldas** in early 2025 with a team of 7.

These three departures in rapid succession stripped the firm of named Legal 500 leading partners in Banking & Finance and Corporate/M&A, and significantly weakened the Mumbai office's corporate transaction capability.
