## Post Text

Most AI tools are built for companies that don't need them. The 78M businesses that do? They can't afford a single one.

Fortune 500 companies have ML teams, data engineers, and six-figure AI budgets. They'll figure it out.

A Coimbatore textile shop owner has a WhatsApp group and an Excel sheet. She won't.

Not because she's not smart enough. Because every AI product on the market assumes she has an IT department.

She doesn't. She has a phone, a UPI QR code, and 14-hour workdays.

59% of Indian MSMEs say budget is the top barrier to AI adoption. But the real barrier is design. Nobody is building AI for the way Indian businesses actually operate.

WhatsApp-first. Hindi-first. Rupee-denominated ROI.

McKinsey says only 6% of companies globally capture real value from AI. The other 94% bought tools built for someone else's workflow.

Indian SMBs don't need another SaaS dashboard. They need an AI employee that lives on WhatsApp, speaks their customers' language, and costs less than a part-time hire.

₹4,999/month. Not ₹50 lakhs/year.

Brutal gap. Massive opportunity.

The AI industry is obsessed with enterprise. We think the real market is 78 million businesses everyone else ignored.

If you're building for Indian SMBs — what's the biggest design assumption you've had to throw out? Genuinely want to know.
