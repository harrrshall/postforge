$500 billion. That's the AI opportunity sitting inside India's 78.3 million MSMEs, according to the World Economic Forum.

Here's what that number looks like for one clinic in Pune.

80 patients a day. One doctor. Two assistants answering WhatsApp messages between consultations. 14 hours of work crammed into 10. Missed calls after 8 PM go unanswered — each one a potential ₹500 consultation lost.

Cleveland Clinic proved AI documentation saves 14 minutes per clinician per day. Seattle Children's cut physician admin workload by 79%.

For a Pune clinic, 14 minutes saved means 2 extra patients seen. At ₹500 each, that's ₹1,000/day. ₹30,000/month. The AI employee costs ₹4,999.

That's a 6x return before you count the WhatsApp messages answered at midnight, the appointment reminders sent automatically, the daily report generated by 7 AM.

But here's what nobody talks about:

Klarna deployed AI customer service, saved $60M, then quietly rehired humans. The winning model wasn't AI-only. It was 60-70% AI, 30-40% human.

Indian SMBs need this hybrid approach from Day 1. Patients want the speed of automation AND the warmth of a real person when it matters.

Real businesses. Real numbers. No AI hype.

What's the one task in your business that eats the most hours every week? Drop it below — I'll tell you if an AI agent can handle it today.
