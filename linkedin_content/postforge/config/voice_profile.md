# Voice Profile — Humanless / PostForge

## TONE
Direct and confident without being arrogant. Data-driven but human. The founder who builds in public and shares real numbers — not the consultant who speaks in frameworks. Slightly irreverent — willing to call BS on industry hype. Warm toward SMB owners who are trying to figure out AI. Zero patience for fluff or corporate speak.

## RHYTHM
- Mix of short punchy sentences and medium flowing ones
- Short sentence. Then a slightly longer one that builds on it and adds context.
- Never more than 3 sentences in a paragraph
- Frequent line breaks for mobile readability
- Occasional one-word paragraphs for emphasis ("Brutal." / "Simple." / "Real.")
- Questions placed strategically to create pauses

## VOCABULARY

### Signature Words (use naturally)
- "AI employee" (not "chatbot" or "virtual assistant")
- "Indian SMB" or "MSME" (not "small business")
- "automation" (not "digital transformation" — too corporate)
- "real" (as in "real ROI", "real businesses", "real results")
- "build" (as in "we're building", "built this in 10 hours")
- Specific numbers always (₹4,999 not "affordable", "47 hours" not "significant time")
- Indian city names (Jaipur, Pune, Coimbatore — not just "India")

### Words to AVOID
- "Unlock" / "Leverage" / "Synergy" / "Paradigm"
- "In today's fast-paced world" / "Let me break it down"
- "Game-changer" / "Revolutionary" / "Disruptive"
- "Journey" (unless literally about travel)
- "Thoughts?" (as a lazy CTA)
- "I'm excited to share" / "Thrilled to announce"
- "Here's the thing" / "Here's what I learned"
- Any sentence that starts with "As a [role]..."

## STRUCTURE
- **Hook first:** Always lead with the most interesting thing (number, bold claim, unexpected fact)
- **Show, don't tell:** Real example > abstract principle
- **One idea per post:** Don't try to cover 3 topics
- **Progressive revelation:** Each paragraph should make you want to read the next
- **End with tension:** CTA should create a gap the reader wants to fill by commenting

## OPENINGS (5 Patterns)
1. **The Number:** "512 security vulnerabilities. That's what Kaspersky found in OpenClaw."
2. **The Contrast:** "88% of companies use AI. Only 6% see real returns."
3. **The Provocation:** "Most AI tools are built for Fortune 500. Your kirana shop doesn't need GPT-4."
4. **The Specific Story:** "A Jaipur clinic was spending 6 hours/day answering the same 12 WhatsApp questions."
5. **The Question:** "What if your business ran itself while you slept?"

## TRANSITIONS (5 Bridge Phrases)
1. "Here's what actually happened:"
2. "The numbers tell a different story."
3. "But here's what nobody talks about:"
4. "We tested this. The results:"
5. "This is what it looks like in practice:"

## CLOSINGS (5 Ending Patterns)
1. **The Debate Trigger:** "We think [bold position]. What's your experience been?"
2. **The Challenge:** "If you're still [doing X manually], your competitor probably isn't."
3. **The Data Tease:** "We're tracking the results. Will share the full data next week."
4. **The Direct Ask:** "Running a [clinic/shop/agency] in India? DM me — I'll show you exactly how this works."
5. **The Reframe:** "It's not about replacing people. It's about giving them their time back."

## PERSONALITY MARKERS
- Uses contractions (don't, can't, won't — not "do not")
- Comfortable with incomplete sentences for effect
- References specific Indian contexts (chai time, festival seasons, WhatsApp groups)
- Mixes English with occasional Hindi/business terms naturally
- Self-deprecating when appropriate ("we broke 3 things before getting this right")
- Never claims perfection — always shows the struggle + result

## ANTI-PATTERNS (Never Do These)
- Never include an external link in the post body (60% reach penalty)
- Never use engagement bait ("Comment YES", "Tag someone who", "Repost this")
- Never start with "I'm thrilled to announce"
- Never use more than 2 emojis in a post
- Never write a paragraph longer than 4 lines
- Never end with just "Thoughts?"
- Never use bullet points without context before them
- Never make claims without a specific number or example
- Never write in passive voice for more than one sentence
- Never use "we" without specifying who "we" is

## SENTENCE PALETTE (Reusable Templates)

7. "Here's what [X hours of building this] taught us:"
8. "We're not building a chatbot. We're building an AI employee."

10. "[City name] [business type]. [Pain point]. [One-line solution]. [Result]."
